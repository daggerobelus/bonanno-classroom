Worksheet 01

Close Reading for Reading

A step-by-step process for approaching a primary source. Work through each step in order — by the end, you'll have the foundation for a claim.

1 Start with the Evidence

Pick a specific passage, quotation, or moment from your primary source. Paste or describe it below. Choose something that strikes you — something confusing, surprising, vivid, or important.

2 Catalogue Your Observations

What do you notice about this passage? Why did you choose it? What stands out about the language, imagery, structure, or tone? List as many observations as you can — don't worry about making an argument yet.

3 Ask "So What?" or "Yes, And?"

Why might your observations be important? What do they tell us that isn't immediately obvious? Push beyond surface-level description — what's interesting or unexpected about what you've noticed?

4 Notice Patterns

Are there other moments in the text that connect to your observations? Do you see repetition, contradiction, development, or tension? How do these patterns deepen your reading?

5 Brainstorm a Claim

Based on your close reading, what did you learn? How has your understanding of the text developed or changed? Try drafting a one- or two-sentence claim that captures your interpretation.

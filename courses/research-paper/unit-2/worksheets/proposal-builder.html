Worksheet 01

Building Your Proposal

Walk through the three-paragraph proposal structure from lecture — research question, sources, and consequences. By the end, you'll have a complete draft proposal.

1 Your Research Question

What is the research question guiding your project? If you completed the Unit 1 worksheet, pull your revised question from there. Your proposal should be built around this question.

2 Identify Your Sources

What primary and secondary sources will you use to answer your research question? List them below, and briefly note what each source contributes to your project.

3 Close Read a Key Passage

Choose a passage from one of your sources and close read it. Provide context for the passage — where does it appear, and why is it significant? Then explain what it reveals about your research question.

4 Articulate the Consequences

Who is the audience for your research? What will they learn or understand differently after reading your paper? Why does this project matter?

5 Draft Your Proposal

Using the work above, draft your full proposal. Aim for three paragraphs: introduce your research question and why it matters, describe your sources and method of analysis, and articulate the consequences of your project.

Worksheet 02

Articulating Consequences

Focused practice on explaining why your research matters. Work through each step to build a clear, compelling consequences paragraph for your proposal.

1 Describe Your Research Project

In one or two sentences, describe what your research project is about. Keep it simple — what are you studying, and what question are you asking?

2 Who Is Your Intended Reader?

Think about who would benefit from reading your research. Is it scholars in a particular field? Students? A general audience? Be as specific as you can about who your reader is and what they already know.

3 What Does Your Research Reveal?

What does your research reveal, demonstrate, or impact? Think beyond summarizing your findings — what new understanding does your project create? What might change as a result?

4 Complete the Sentence

Use this sentence frame to sharpen your thinking. Complete the sentence below — this can serve as the core claim of your consequences paragraph.

5 Draft Your Consequences Paragraph

Using your work above, draft a full consequences paragraph for your proposal. Explain who your audience is, what your research reveals, and why it matters.

Worksheet 03

Rough Draft Builder

Plan and draft your rough draft argument for Science, Fiction, and Technology. This worksheet walks you through your main claim, three body paragraphs, and the coversheet reflection.

1 Your Main Claim

Write the main claim for your research paper. This is your thesis statement — the argument you'll be making loudly and proudly in your introduction. A strong main claim will be specific, contestable, significant, and supportable. If you completed the Evaluating Your Claim worksheet, use your revised claim from there.

2 Body Paragraph One

Plan and draft your first body paragraph. Remember: your subclaim should argue a smaller chunk of your main claim — you don't need to prove everything at once.

3 Body Paragraph Two

Plan and draft your second body paragraph. Think about how this paragraph builds on or develops the argument from your first paragraph.

4 Body Paragraph Three

Plan and draft your third body paragraph. Consider how all three paragraphs work together to support your main claim.

5 Coversheet Reflection

Answer the following questions to include at the top of your rough draft submission. This is a rough draft — it's supposed to be messy, unpolished, and incomplete. The goal is to practice argumentation skills and get started on your research paper.

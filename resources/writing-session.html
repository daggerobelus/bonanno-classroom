Focused Writing Session

A self-guided write-in to help you set a goal, get words on the page, and reflect on your progress.

1 Choose Your Writing Goal

Before you start writing, spend a few minutes thinking about what you want to accomplish in this session. No goal is too small — and a focused goal is better than a vague one. Check any goals below that apply, or write your own.

Read and interpret the prompt
Annotate the prompt, identify what's being asked, and note any questions you have.
Choose some evidence
Find passages or sources to work with. Type potential quotations into one document.
Free write
Get words on the page without worrying about quality. Write your way to a main claim or idea.
Outline your thoughts
Create a bullet-point list or mind map to give your draft a sense of direction.
Draft a section of your paper
Write one paragraph or section — breaking up the work makes it less overwhelming.
Revise your main claim
Look for a stronger version of your thesis — often hiding in your conclusion or later paragraphs.
Reverse outline your argument
Identify the point and function of each paragraph. Look for repetition or unclear logic.
Polish and proofread
Check citations, fix grammar and spelling, and make sure your evidence is properly cited.
2 Set Your Timer

Research shows that writers are most productive when they write in short, focused chunks — even as little as 30 minutes. Set a timer for however long feels right (25, 30, 45, or 60 minutes), get something to keep you hydrated, put on some study music, and find a comfortable spot.

3 Write

This is your time. Execute your goal — write without judgment or expectations. If you get stuck, come back to your goal and try to focus on just one small piece of it. You can use the space below for notes, or work directly in your document.

4 Reflect on Your Session

After your writing time is up, take a few minutes to reflect. Honest reflection helps you understand your writing habits and improve over time.

What did you accomplish today?

It's OK and very normal if you didn't accomplish everything you set out to do.

What worked well? What would you do differently next time?

What are you happy about with your writing so far? What are you less happy about?

What questions do you still have about your paper?

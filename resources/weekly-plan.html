Build Your Weekly Plan

Map out the big picture, then zoom in on one assignment. Use this worksheet each week to build a sustainable work routine.

1 Your Fixed Commitments

What's already set in stone this week? List your classes, work shifts, meetings, appointments, and any personal obligations.

2 Your Peak Hours

Not all hours are equal. When do you do your best, most focused work? Morning? Afternoon? Late evening? When do you hit a wall? Knowing this helps you schedule hard tasks at the right time.

3 What's Due This Week

List everything that's due this week across all your classes. Include small things (readings, posts) and big things (papers, exams). Getting it all in one place helps you see the full picture.

4 Block Your Time

Based on your fixed commitments, peak hours, and deadlines — when will you work on what? Schedule your hardest tasks during your best hours. Don't forget to block time for eating, breaks, and social life.

5 Pick One Assignment to Plan

Choose the assignment that feels biggest or most overwhelming this week. What is it, and when is it due?

6 Break It Down

Work backwards from the due date. What are the smaller steps you need to complete? For a paper, this might be: choose a passage, close read, draft a thesis, write the body, revise. List the steps and estimate how long each one takes.

7 Schedule Your Sessions

Using the Pomodoro Technique (25-30 minute focused sessions with breaks), plan when you'll work on each step. Which days? What time? Try to spread the work across multiple days rather than cramming it all into one.

8 Your Accountability Plan

Who can help keep you on track? A friend you'll exchange drafts with? A study group? A self-imposed deadline? We follow through more often when someone else is involved. Name your person and set a check-in point.
